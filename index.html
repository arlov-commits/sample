Start Safely · six rules that protect your knees

🔥Warm up firstNever fold cold hips. Spend ~5 min in Butterfly and gentle knee rocks before any lotus shape.
🪑Lift your seatSit on a firm cushion so the knees settle at or below hip level — it tilts the pelvis and frees the hips.
🦴Turn from the hipThe opening is external rotation of the hip joint. The knee is a hinge — it must never twist to get there.
⚖️Even both sidesAlternate which leg sits on top each session so the hips open symmetrically.
🌬️Breathe & softenExhale and let gravity do the work. Forcing closes the hips; patient, relaxed time opens them.
🚪Know the exitRelease slowly the instant a red-flag signal appears. Backing out early is how you progress faster.

Body Signals · learn the difference before you deepen

🟢 Good — keep going (ease off if it gets intense)

  • A broad, warm stretch across the hips, groin or inner thighs
  • The sensation softens as you breathe and relax into it
  • Mild muscle fatigue that fades within a day — ordinary soreness
  • Both sides feel roughly similar over the weeks

🔴 Stop now — back out slowly

  • Sharp, pinching or shooting pain — especially in the knee
  • Any pain inside the knee joint or around the kneecap
  • Numbness, tingling or "pins & needles" in the feet or legs
  • Pain that lingers or worsens hours or a day later
  • Clicking with pain, swelling, or a feeling of instability

Rule of thumb: muscles stretch (dull, broad, tolerable) — joints should not (sharp, pinpoint = danger). Knee pain is never part of lotus. When in doubt, ease out.

🩹 Felt soreness after a sit? Gentle, dull muscle soreness for a day or two after new stretching is normal — move lightly and hydrate. Knee pain, joint pain or swelling is not — rest it, drop back a stage, and don't push through. Sharp or lasting knee pain means see a physio before continuing.

Educational guidance from your Lotus Path dashboard — not medical advice. If you have a knee, hip or back condition, check with a professional before practising.

Your 5-Week Foundation Program · ~10–15 min, 5–6 days a week

1

Settle & Assess

~10 min
  • 5 min seated breathing in Easy Pose on a firm cushion
  • 5 min Butterfly — soften the groin, no bouncing
  • Note your baseline: how high do the knees float?
🎯 Sit 5 min calm and mark your starting point.
2

Open the Hips

~12 min
  • Warm up: 4 min Butterfly + slow knee rocks
  • Reclined figure-4 (ankle over knee), 1–2 min each side
  • 6 min Easy Pose, spine tall, alternate the top shin
🎯 Knees drop lower in Butterfly; sit 8 min with ease.
3

Meet Quarter Lotus

~13 min
  • Warm up 5 min (Butterfly + figure-4)
  • Quarter Lotus — foot on the opposite calf, 1–2 min each side
  • Finish in Easy Pose, switching the top leg
🎯 Quarter Lotus 2 min per side, fully pain-free.
4

Toward Half Lotus

~15 min
  • Warm up + 1–2 min gentle Pigeon each side
  • Quarter Lotus held up to 5 min per side
  • Test Half Lotus only if the knee rests low: 30–60 s/side, then release
🎯 Easy Quarter Lotus 5 min; a calm taste of Half Lotus.
5

Consolidate & Balance

~15 min
  • Full warm-up: Butterfly, figure-4, Pigeon
  • Half Lotus building both sides, 1–3 min per side
  • Close with 5 min still in your best comfortable pose
🎯 Half Lotus both sides, no knee strain — foundation set.

🌱 Reality check: most bodies need months, not weeks, to reach Full Lotus safely. Repeat Weeks 3–5 as a cycle until Half Lotus is effortless on both sides — then Padmasana follows. Patience is the practice.
